Jonathan Taylor



My name is Jonathan Taylor. I am a student pursuing a B.S. degree in computer science at Oregon Status University while I work full-time in the airlines and pursue an intentional, laughter-filled life with my wife.



I have always been drawn to solving problems and creating solutions in all parts of my life. This enthusiasm has given me great success and satisfaction in my current occupation as an Aircraft Technician at Envoy Air Inc. As an aircraft technician, I perform a great deal of troubleshooting and problem solving to fix discrepancies on the aircraft I work with.



My prompting to pursue a different vocation has stemmed from my desire for continual development, my love of learning, and a healthy dose of frustration with the reactionary nature of fixing aircraft discrepancies caused by design flaws. My desire is to educate myself and move to the level of an engineer who designs the systems, prevents the problems, and adjusts the architecture of a design to continually improve the system. I have always enjoyed building personal computers, tinkering with Arduino code, and maintaining the aircraft flight computers I work with. So, I begin this journey with the hope that computer science is a field where I can accel.



My attitude and diligence towards both my current vocation, and my schoolwork are driven by my core values:

  • Work to do the best job I can the first time, learn from the failures, and then make each attempt that follows even better.
  • Cultivate a lifestyle of working for excellence, investing in my personal relationships, and recognizing when I mistakenly place my worth in either one.
  • My attitude towards each task or situation has more of an impact on my response than the circumstances.



I am still at the stage of constantly amazement at how much software is out there that I do not know yet, and that is where I find the excitement. Even though sometimes I spend hours searching for one misplaced semicolon, the struggle is part of the journey. There is an entire world of software development and engineering to be discovered, and I am loving my lifelong journey of discovery in it.
